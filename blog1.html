Innovative Use of Version 3 Electrolyser: Creating Rubies from Aluminum Foil

At HydrogenResearch, innovation is at the heart of everything we do. We’re thrilled to share our latest adventure in the world of science—using our electrolyser to transform ordinary aluminum foil into dazzling synthetic rubies. Yes, you read that right! Our ongoing experiment is a testament to the incredible versatility of our HHO (Hydrogen-Oxygen) technology.

Our journey begins with something as common as aluminum foil. But don’t be fooled by its everyday nature; this simple material is about to undergo a fascinating metamorphosis.

From Foil to Fuel: We start by carefully cleaning the aluminum foil to eliminate any contaminants that could interfere with our experiment. The foil is then cut into small pieces to maximize the surface area, ensuring that the upcoming reactions are as efficient as possible.

Through electrolysis, the aluminum foil encounters a concentrated solution of sodium hydroxide (NaOH), as the aluminum reacts with the sodium hydroxide to produce aluminum hydroxide (Al(OH)₃) and release hydrogen gas (H₂):

2Al + 6NaOH + 6H₂O → 2Na₃AlO₃ + 3H₂

To convert our liquid sodium aluminate into solid aluminum hydroxide, we neutralize the solution with sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄). This process precipitates aluminum hydroxide out of the solution:

Na₃AlO₃ + 3H₂SO₄ → Al(OH)₃ + 3Na₂SO₄

Next, we heat the aluminum hydroxide to high temperatures (around 1000-1100°C) in a kiln. This stage, known as calcination, drives off the water and turns the aluminum hydroxide into pure alumina (Al₂O₃):

2Al(OH)₃ → Al₂O₃ + 3H₂O

Creating the Sparkling Rubies

From Alumina to Rubies: The purified alumina is then subjected to intense heat in a controlled environment to simulate the natural conditions under which rubies are formed. We add a touch of chromium (CrO₃) to the alumina to give it that iconic red hue. The result? Stunning synthetic rubies that shine with the same brilliance as their natural counterparts.

Our experiment is still in the works, but the early results are nothing short of exciting. This groundbreaking project not only demonstrates the fpotential of our electrolyser.
